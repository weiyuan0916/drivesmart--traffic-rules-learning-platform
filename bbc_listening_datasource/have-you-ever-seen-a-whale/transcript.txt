4. What did you learn that was surprising or new in this episode?
5. Answer the quiz questions. Listen again or use the transcript to help you.
1. Why have whales been hunted throughout history?
a) for sport
b) to protect fish supplies
c) for their oil, meat and bones
2. Which word means the 'temporary suspension of an activity'?
a) museum
b) moratorium
c) mausoleum
3. "Jenny's team had lost 10 matches in a row, when they unexpectedly beat
the league champions! That was the _____ in their fortunes."
a) turning point
b) turning round
c) turning back
4. How fast can blue whales swim?
a) up to 30km/h
b) up to 40km/h
c) up to 50km/h
5. Which phrase means to 'publicly praise someone'?
a) give a shout-at
b) give a handout
c) give a shout-out
6. What is an informal way of saying 'man'?
a) kid
b) chap
c) dear

Answers
3.
a. moratorium – temporary suspension of an activity
b. chap – (informal) man
c. turning point – time when a situation starts to change, especially in a
positive way
d. (not) stand a chance – (not) have a chance of success
e. give a shout-out – publicly praise someone or something
f. good at media – skilful at using media outlets, like TV and newspapers, to
convey a message to the public
5. 1c, 2b, 3a, 4a, 5c, 6b

Teacher's notes
This worksheet is standardised so that you can amend and use it as appropriate
with your classes.
Some topics may be difficult for learners, especially those which could be
upsetting or cause offence. Please be mindful of this when using this worksheet,
and adapt it as necessary.
Extension activities
• Role-play an interview with an 'interviewer' and an 'expert' on this topic.
• Write a short story or article using the vocabulary from this episode.
• Read original articles on this topic.
• For homework: If it is available in your country, download and listen to the
original podcast these extracts were taken from.
